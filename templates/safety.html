Pesticide Safety Guidelines

Important information for safe and effective pesticide application.

The label is the law. It contains critical information about mixing, application, required personal protective equipment (PPE), and first aid. Never use a pesticide in a manner inconsistent with its labeling.

At a minimum, always wear long sleeves, long pants, closed-toe shoes, and chemical-resistant gloves. Depending on the product, you may also need eye protection (goggles), a respirator, or a chemical-resistant apron.

Mix pesticides in a well-ventilated area, away from children, pets, and food sources. Never eat, drink, or smoke while handling pesticides. Wash your hands thoroughly with soap and water after use and before using the restroom.

Store pesticides in their original, labeled containers in a locked, secure cabinet away from extreme temperatures. Never pour leftover pesticides down the drain or into the storm sewer. Follow local guidelines for hazardous waste disposal.
Disclaimer: This information is for general guidance only. Always refer to the product label and local agricultural extension services for specific instructions.
